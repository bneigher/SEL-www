Ms. Gini's 1st Classroom

Students identified communal classroom areas such as the rug space as a Green Space. However, areas such as our Library needed to have clearer expectations, as seen with the Pink Space.

Ms. Gini's 2nd Classroom

Students defined Pink Spaces as a “sad face,” or areas that did not make us happy. Table gropus are where students are for gropu projects and independent work, which make up a lot of class time. Check out our blog post about how this classroom teacher used maps to have meaningful discussions and create changes in her classroom with students.
Ms. Gini's 3rd Classroom

Students agreed that desks were a Green Space. We co-defined Green Spaces as places where “you can be yourself” and where we can feel 'comfortable.'
